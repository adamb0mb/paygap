The Gender Pay Gap
The national median salary for men in any job is $50,254 and for women it is $41,474. That's a difference of $8,870 per year! (17.5% lower) (reference).

The "controlled" salaries are a true apples-to-apples comparison (or as close as techincally possible), and the pay gap is much smaller in each individual job. So, what is the difference? There are more men in higher paying jobs (engineering, etc) and there are more women in lower paying jobs (office assistant, etc).

"Uncontrolled" and "controlled" salaries can be so different, why? Typically this happens because the men in that job have more years experience than women (or vice versa).

This is the roughly how we compute it: (1) for each woman in our data, we find a man with a similar education, location, experience level, etc; (2) the woman goes in to the "controlled" female group and the man goes into the "controlled" male group; (3) then we compute the median salary of these two groups.
