About the PayGap Explorer

We heard about a competition sponsored by the U.S Department of Labor to help promote education about the gender pay gap.
 

What are we doing?

We believe there is more to the story than just "men are paid more, women are paid less." We did some research and found more nuance to the subject than that. After looking researching and reporting on compensation data, we've discovered a couple of points, and wanted to share them with the world.
  • When you look at a group of equivalent men and women in a given job, the pay is usually only skewed in men's favor by single digits.
  • Women seem to end up in lower-paying jobs

How are we doing it?

We are using free and/or open source tools to produce this: jQuery, History.js, Google Charts, CDNJS, and GitHub

PayScale has internal tools to do complex mathematical and statistical calculations. Unforunately, we can't release those to the public, but we are releasing the resulting data. It's available on the github repo for PayGap.
 

Who are we

Well, first off, we all work at PayScale, a company specializing in salary data collection and reporting. Every so often, the whole development team gets together, and we each work on any project that we want to. This time, we put together a little team, and came up with this.
